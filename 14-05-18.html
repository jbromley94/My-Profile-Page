Captains log: Stardate : 14/05/18

Welcome!
Hello, you've made it to my first ever blog!



Today was my first day at Northcoders!
I have already been pushed a bit.

We started the day off with a brief intro to the course and introducing ourself to our fellow cohorts. This was led by Chris. One of the founders. He has background working for Sky and a few others.
It wasn't long before we were being led by Paul, our tutor for this block, lasting 2 weeks. With a brief introduction to how JavaScript works - it tackles lines one by one. We covered execution context before moving onto the importance of pair programming.
We learnt the respective roles of each person in the pair - the driver and the navigator. The driver mainly writes the code. The navigator proposing the solutions and thought processes.
From there we covered test driven development. This is where we write tests for our code to pass. Mainly upon each important iteration.
After an hour break at 1 we came back to be let loose on our own coding challenges and to write our own tests for the challenges.
In all honesty it was hard! It was a new way of coding which none of us had previous experience with. Sometimes it felt like we were trying to catch ourselves out.
My partner, Nichola, and I made our way through a few of them before departing at 6.

What a fantastic start to our first day :D

To summarise: Although I was originally naturally a bit apprehensive about what we would study and how ready I was, I feel I have already found a good few friends and a great opportunity .

Jordan


P.S Thank God it's light in the mornings. At least getting up will be a bit easier.
